💡 Meditation Tips

Practical guidance for building a lasting practice

🌅

Best Time to Meditate

Early morning (Brahma Muhurta, 4–6 AM) is considered ideal in yoga tradition — the mind is fresh, the world is quiet, and the energy is sattvic (pure). Even 10 minutes post-wake-up is powerful.

🪑

Posture & Seat

Sit with your spine erect — this keeps you alert without tension. Use a cushion or folded blanket under your hips. Keep your chin slightly tucked. Crossed legs, kneeling, or a chair all work equally well.

🧠

Mind Wandering is Normal

Every time you notice your mind has wandered and gently bring it back — that IS the meditation. Beginners may redirect 100 times in 10 minutes. That's not failure; that's strength-training for attention.

⏱️

Start Small, Stay Consistent

5 minutes every day beats 60 minutes once a week. Neuroscience shows that consistent short sessions build the meditative habit faster. Use your breathing session above as a natural entry point.

👁️

Eyes: Open or Closed?

Closed eyes deepen inward awareness. Slightly open, downward-gazing eyes (Zen style) prevent drowsiness. Try both — most people find eyes closed works better for beginners.

🌬️

Anchor to the Breath

When starting out, simply observe the sensation of breath at your nostrils — the slight coolness on inhale, warmth on exhale. This physical anchor is more reliable than counting or mantras for most beginners.

🧘 Yoga + Meditation Together

Why combining movement and stillness multiplies benefits

⚡

Movement Prepares the Mind

Physical yoga (asana) burns restless energy and releases muscle tension, making it far easier to sit still and focus during meditation immediately after.

🫀

Deeper Parasympathetic Response

Yoga activates the vagus nerve through breathing and posture. Meditation deepens this response. Together, cortisol drops 30–40% more than either practice alone.

🔁

The Ideal Sequence

Practice 20–40 min of asana → 5 min of Savasana (Corpse Pose) → 10–20 min of seated meditation. The body's stillness after yoga makes meditation effortless.

🌱

Long-Term Brain Changes

After 8 weeks of combined practice, MRI studies show measurable thickening of the insula and prefrontal cortex — regions governing self-awareness, empathy, and emotional regulation.
